COVID 19 Tips

Coronavirus commonly known as Covid 19 is caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. This viral infection is extremely contagious but majority of people will recover while a small a small percentage of people will require medical attention. People with pre-existing conditions, for examples the elderly, people with asthma, diabetes are mostly likely to require medical attention as the virus further weakens their immune system. Protect yourself and others from infection by staying at least 1 meter apart from others, wearing a properly fitted mask, and washing your hands or using an alcohol-based rub frequently the virus can be spread from an infected person’s mouth or nose in small liquid particles when they cough, sneeze, speak, sing or breathe. Get vaccinated to protect yourself and your loved ones.

SA Unemployment rate falls to 6.4%

The country's unemployment rate declined for the second consecutive quarter, according to Stats SA's Quarterly Labour Force Survey (QLFS) for the second quarter of the year. Although only slightly, the nation's unemployment rate decreased from 34.5% in the first quarter to 33.9% in the second, a change of 0.6 percentage points. Community and Social Services (276,000), Trade (169,000), Finance (128,000), and Construction saw the largest increases in employment (104,000). However, jobs in manufacturing (73,000) and transportation were lost (54,000). In the second quarter of 2022, there were 15.6 million people who were employed overall. The Western Cape has the lowest rate using the enlarged definition of unemployment at 31.3%, whereas the Northern Cape has the lowest rate by the standard definition at 23.7%.
